Type: T
Title: The Whispering Forest
Author: NYCU Library Team

In the northern mountains, there was a forest that no traveler dared to cross at night. The villagers called it the Whispering Forest because strange voices could always be heard between the trees whenever the wind became strong. Some people believed the forest was haunted, while others claimed the sounds were simply the echoes of nature.

A young student named Ethan did not believe in ghost stories. During his summer vacation, he decided to travel alone and investigate the mystery himself. Carrying only a lantern, a notebook, and a small backpack, he entered the forest before sunset.

At first, everything seemed peaceful. The trees were tall and covered the sky like a giant green roof. Small birds flew above him, and the ground was covered with soft leaves. Ethan carefully wrote down everything he saw because he wanted to publish a travel journal after returning home.

As night slowly approached, the atmosphere changed completely. The forest became darker and colder. Ethan began hearing soft whispers from somewhere far away. The sounds were unclear, almost like people speaking in another language.

Although he felt nervous, Ethan continued walking deeper into the woods. Suddenly, he discovered an old wooden cabin hidden behind the trees. The windows were broken, and the door moved slowly with the wind.

Inside the cabin, he found dozens of books scattered across the floor. Most of them were damaged by rain and time, but one book remained completely clean. The title on the cover read: “The Memory of the Forest.”

Ethan opened the book carefully. To his surprise, every page described events that had happened inside the forest many years ago. Some pages even mentioned travelers who had disappeared without explanation.

Then Ethan noticed something impossible.

The final page was still blank.

As he stared at the empty paper, words slowly appeared by themselves.

“They are listening to you now.”

The whispering outside the cabin suddenly became louder.

Ethan quickly closed the book and rushed outside, but the forest no longer looked the same. The trees seemed taller, and the paths had completely disappeared.

For hours he wandered through the darkness while the whispers surrounded him from every direction. Just before sunrise, he finally saw the edge of the forest and escaped.

When Ethan returned to the village, nobody believed his story. However, from that day onward, he could still hear faint whispers every night whenever the wind blew through his window.
