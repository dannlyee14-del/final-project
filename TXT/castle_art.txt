Title: Kingdom of ASCII
Author: NYCU Art Group

Long ago, there existed a kingdom built entirely from symbols and imagination. Travelers from distant lands visited the capital city not because of its wealth, but because of the magnificent castle standing at the center of the kingdom.

The castle was protected by brave knights who trained every day inside the stone courtyard. The king believed knowledge was more important than power, so the royal library became the largest building after the castle itself.

Every child in the kingdom learned how to read before learning how to fight. Artists created paintings using only letters and symbols, while engineers designed mechanical machines powered entirely by water.

One winter night, a mysterious traveler arrived carrying an ancient map. According to the map, a hidden treasure was buried beneath the castle. However, the treasure was not gold or jewels.

Deep underground, hidden behind a secret door, the traveler discovered a giant chamber filled with books containing forgotten stories from ancient civilizations. The knowledge stored there was considered more valuable than any treasure in history.

The king decided to open the underground library to the public so everyone could learn from the wisdom of the past. From that day forward, the Kingdom of ASCII became famous across the world as the land where imagination and knowledge ruled together.
