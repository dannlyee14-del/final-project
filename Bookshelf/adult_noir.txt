| MIDNIGHT NOIR   |
|_________________|
.figend

Rain fell over Blackwater City like a curtain of silver needles.

The streets reflected neon lights from bars, casinos, and late-night diners. Most citizens were asleep, but Detective Marcus Kane knew the city revealed its true nature after midnight.

Crime never slept.

Neither did Marcus.

For ten years he had investigated murders, robberies, and corruption. He thought he had seen everything.

Then the Midnight Case arrived.

It began with a phone call at 12:07 a.m.

A nightclub owner named Victor Hale had been found dead in his private office.

Marcus arrived twenty minutes later.

The nightclub was crowded with police officers and frightened employees.

Victor sat behind his desk.

There were no signs of struggle.

No broken furniture.

No weapon.

Only a small piece of paper resting beside his hand.

Marcus picked up the note.

Written across it was a strange message.

.... . .-.. .--.

Most officers ignored it.

Marcus did not.

Years earlier he had learned Morse code while serving in the military.

The message translated to:

HELP

Why would a dying man leave such a message?

That question haunted Marcus.

The victim's business records revealed something unusual.

Victor Hale had recently transferred large amounts of money into several anonymous accounts.

Someone had been paying him.

Or blackmailing him.

Marcus spent the next week following the money trail.

Every lead pointed toward the same location.

The Crimson Crown Casino.

| CRIMSON CROWN |

The casino was owned by Leonard Graves.

Officially, Graves was a businessman.

Unofficially, everyone knew he controlled much of Blackwater's criminal underworld.

Yet nobody had ever proven it.

Marcus visited the casino posing as a customer.

Inside, the atmosphere was luxurious.

Music played softly.

Gamblers laughed around card tables.

Waitresses moved between crowds carrying expensive drinks.

Everything appeared normal.

Too normal.

As Marcus explored the building, he discovered a hidden office behind the manager's room.

Inside he found ledgers filled with coded transactions.

One page contained a simple equation.

?20+22?

It seemed meaningless.

Yet Marcus copied it into his notebook.

Later that night he reviewed the evidence.

The number appeared repeatedly throughout the records.

Every suspicious transaction referenced the same value.

Someone was using mathematical codes to communicate.

| SECRET RECORDS |

A breakthrough came when an informant named Sarah contacted him.

She claimed Victor Hale had been preparing to expose a corruption network involving politicians, police officers, and business leaders.

Before she could reveal more, someone attempted to kill her.

Marcus barely managed to get her to safety.

Now he knew the conspiracy was real.

Someone powerful wanted the truth buried.

Over the next few days, Marcus and Sarah worked together.

The coded transactions eventually revealed a pattern.

The numbers corresponded to warehouse locations throughout the city.

One warehouse stood out.

Warehouse 42.

The same number hidden inside the casino records.

The same number connected to Victor's final investigation.

.fig
______________
| WAREHOUSE 42|
|_____________|
.figend

On Friday night, Marcus entered the warehouse alone.

Rows of shipping containers filled the massive building.

At first it seemed abandoned.

Then voices echoed from the upper level.

Marcus climbed quietly.

From a balcony he observed a secret meeting.

Politicians.

Business executives.

Crime bosses.

All gathered in one place.

Among them stood Leonard Graves.

The meeting confirmed everything.

The warehouse served as the headquarters of an organized corruption network.

Marcus activated a hidden recorder.

Every conversation became evidence.

Suddenly a loose metal pipe rolled across the floor.

The sound echoed through the building.

Everyone turned.

Marcus had been discovered.

The next several minutes became chaos.

Gunfire erupted.

Marcus sprinted through the warehouse while criminals pursued him.

He narrowly escaped through a loading bay and transmitted the evidence to police headquarters.

By sunrise, arrest warrants had been issued.

The corruption network collapsed within days.

Leonard Graves disappeared before police could capture him.

Many believed he fled the country.

Others believed he was murdered by former associates.

No one knew the truth.

Weeks later, Marcus visited Victor Hale's grave.

The case had finally been solved.

The city was safer.

But one mystery remained.

Who had truly written the final Morse code message?

Victor?

Or someone else?

As Marcus turned to leave, he noticed a fresh piece of paper resting against the gravestone.

Four familiar symbols were written across it.

.... . .-.. .--.

HELP.

Marcus stared at the message.

The Midnight Case was over.

But another mystery had already begun.

THE END
