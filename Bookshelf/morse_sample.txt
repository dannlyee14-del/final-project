In the year 2026, Agent Ryan joined the International Signal Agency.

His mission was simple.

Decode messages.

Protect information.

Save lives.

Most people communicated using modern networks.

However, some secret transmissions still relied on Morse code.

.fig
        .----.
       /      \
      | RADIO |
       \______/
.figend

On his first day, Ryan received a message.

.... . .-.. .-.. ---

The transmission was weak.

But Ryan recognized it immediately.

The message said hello.

The sender was testing the communication channel.

.fig
      ________________
     | MESSAGE #001  |
     |________________|
.figend

Several hours later, another message arrived.

-. -.-- -.-. ..-

The agency computer began searching its database.

The decoded result identified a university.

The sender was located nearby.

Ryan logged the message and continued monitoring.

.fig
      _____________
     | DECODING... |
     |_____________|
.figend

Days passed quietly.

Then a priority signal arrived.

... --- ...

Everyone in the control room froze.

SOS.

A distress signal.

Somewhere, someone needed help.

Ryan immediately traced the source.

The signal originated from a research vessel in the Pacific Ocean.

.fig
          ~~~~~~~
       ~~~ SHIP ~~~
      ~~~~~~~~~~~~~
.figend

The ship had lost power during a storm.

Its communication systems were damaged.

Only a simple Morse transmitter remained operational.

Ryan relayed the coordinates to rescue teams.

Hours later, the crew was rescued.

The captain later sent a thank-you message.

.... . .-.. .-.. ---

HELLO

The same signal that began Ryan's first day.

.fig
       \o/
        |
       / \
.figend

Months later, Ryan discovered a strange encrypted transmission.

--- --- .--.

The message repeated every night.

Nobody knew who was sending it.

Nobody knew why.

The agency assigned Ryan to investigate.

.fig
     ___________________
    | UNKNOWN SIGNAL   |
    |__________________|
.figend

The trail led him to an abandoned observatory.

Inside he found an old radio station still operating.

A computer terminal displayed a challenge.

?20+26?

Ryan solved it instantly.

The screen unlocked.

A hidden file appeared.

.fig
      ____________
     | TERMINAL  |
     |___________|
.figend

Inside the file was a collection of historical Morse transmissions.

Messages from explorers.

Scientists.

Astronauts.

Emergency crews.

The archive preserved decades of communication history.

One message caught Ryan's attention.

.... . .-.. .--.

HELP

The message dated back more than fifty years.

Nobody knew who sent it.

.fig
    ____________________
   | HISTORICAL ARCHIVE |
   |____________________|
.figend

As Ryan continued exploring, he encountered additional security puzzles.

?12*12?

?sqrt(144)?

?power(2,7)?

Each correct answer unlocked another section of the archive.

The deeper he went, the more history he uncovered.

.fig
      [ ACCESS ]
      ███░░░░░░
.figend

Finally, he reached the last record.

It contained a message from the founder of the agency.

"Technology changes."

"Communication evolves."

"But understanding one another remains humanity's greatest achievement."

Ryan smiled.

The founder was right.

Whether through telegraphs, radios, satellites, or computers, the goal was always the same.

Connecting people.

.fig
      __________________
     | CONNECTION IS   |
     | OUR STRENGTH    |
     |_________________|
.figend

Before leaving, Ryan sent one final transmission.

.... . .-.. .-.. ---

The signal traveled across the world.

And somewhere, another operator received it.

THE END
