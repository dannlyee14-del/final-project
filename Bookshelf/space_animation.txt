Year 2150.

Humanity had finally achieved what generations of scientists had dreamed of for centuries.

The stars were no longer unreachable.

The spacecraft Starlight One stood proudly on Launch Platform 7, preparing for the first interstellar mission in human history.

Thousands of engineers, scientists, and reporters gathered to witness the event.

Captain Elena Morris stood inside the command deck, reviewing the final systems check.

"Navigation systems online."

"Life support online."

"Quantum drive ready."

Everything was prepared.

At exactly 08:00 UTC, mission control issued the final command.

"Starlight One, you are cleared for launch."

A deep rumble shook the ground.

Flames erupted beneath the spacecraft.

The rocket slowly rose into the sky.

The crowd cheered.

Humanity's greatest journey had begun.

For the first few days, everything proceeded according to plan.

The crew adjusted to life in deep space.

They conducted experiments, monitored systems, and observed distant stars.

Outside the observation window, countless points of light filled the darkness.

Some were stars.

Some were planets.

Some were galaxies millions of light-years away.

One evening, while scanning nearby space, the ship's radar detected something unusual.

"Captain," said Lieutenant Harris.

"We have an unidentified object."

The object was enormous.

Far larger than any spacecraft ever built by humans.

Even stranger, it appeared completely motionless.

The crew changed course.

As Starlight One approached, the object slowly emerged from the darkness.

It was an ancient space station.

No known records mentioned such a structure.

No signals were being transmitted.

No lights were visible.

Yet the station remained perfectly intact.

After careful discussion, Captain Morris approved an exploration mission.

A small team boarded a shuttle and traveled toward the station.

The docking bay opened automatically.

No one knew why.

No one knew who built it.

And no one knew what they were about to discover.

Inside the station, the walls were covered with symbols unlike any language ever seen on Earth.

Blue lights illuminated endless corridors.

Strange machines hummed quietly.

The station seemed alive.

Yet there was nobody there.

The deeper they explored, the more questions appeared.

At the center of the station stood a giant spherical chamber.

Floating above the floor was a glowing map of the galaxy.

Thousands of star systems appeared as points of light.

Then the map changed.

A new route appeared.

A route leading far beyond any known region of space.

Captain Morris stared silently.

Humanity had just discovered evidence of an advanced civilization.

And perhaps...

an invitation.
