Welcome to the Wrap Demo Laboratory.

This special laboratory was built to test the interaction between pictures, stories, and puzzles.

Researchers from around the world visited the lab to see how text could flow around images while still remaining easy to read.

One day, a young engineer named Maya arrived at the laboratory.

She had heard rumors about a hidden room somewhere inside the building.

According to legend, the room could only be opened by solving a series of challenges.

Maya entered the first hallway.

At the end of the corridor she found a digital panel.

A message appeared:

?15+27?

The answer illuminated the hallway lights.

The first door opened.

Maya smiled.

"Looks like this place likes puzzles."

Old radios, telegraphs, and computer terminals filled the space.

Suddenly one terminal began printing strange symbols.

.... . .-.. .-.. ---

HELLO.

The machine beeped happily.

The passage led to the Puzzle Chamber.

Three stone tablets stood in the center.

The first displayed:

?12*12?

The second displayed:

?sqrt(81)?

The third displayed:

?power(2,6)?

Each solved puzzle activated another mechanism.

Gears turned.

Lights flashed.

Ancient machines awakened.

The final room contained a giant archive.

Thousands of ASCII artworks covered the walls.

Cats.

Castles.

Spaceships.

Dragons.

Every image had been preserved for future generations.

In the center stood a golden plaque.

It contained one final challenge.

?50*2+100/5?

The moment the answer appeared, the archive doors opened completely.

Inside the archive Maya discovered the laboratory's true purpose.

It had never been built merely to store pictures.

It existed to preserve creativity.

Every drawing.

Every equation.

Every message.

Every story.

All were forms of communication.

Before leaving, Maya wrote a message in the visitor log:

"Pictures inspire imagination."

"Numbers inspire understanding."

"Messages connect people."

As she stepped outside, the laboratory lights slowly dimmed.

The Wrap Demo Laboratory would continue waiting for the next curious visitor.
