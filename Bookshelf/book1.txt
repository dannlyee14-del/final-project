Long ago, in a peaceful valley surrounded by green hills and forests, there lived three little pigs with their mother.

Their small cottage was warm and comfortable, but as the pigs grew older, their mother knew it was time for them to start their own lives.

"My children," she said one morning, "the world is full of opportunities, but it is also full of dangers. You must learn to take care of yourselves."

The three pigs listened carefully.

The first pig was cheerful but lazy.

The second pig was clever but impatient.

The third pig was hardworking and thoughtful.

The next day, the three pigs packed their belongings and left home.

Each pig planned to build a house.

The first pig walked only a short distance before meeting a farmer carrying a bundle of straw.

"May I have some straw?" asked the pig.

"Of course," replied the farmer.

The first pig quickly built a straw house.

It took less than one afternoon.

"Perfect!" he said happily.

Then he spent the rest of the day sleeping.

Meanwhile, the second pig met a woodcutter carrying sticks.

"May I have some wood?" he asked.

The woodcutter agreed.

The second pig worked harder than his brother.

Within two days, he completed a wooden house.

"Strong enough," he said proudly.

He then spent his time fishing and exploring the river.

The third pig traveled much farther.

Eventually, he reached a village where builders were constructing homes from brick.

The pig carefully studied their work.

He purchased bricks and began building.

Day after day he worked.

While his brothers relaxed, he continued laying bricks.

The project took weeks.

But when it was finished, the house looked magnificent.

One evening, a hungry wolf entered the valley.

The wolf had sharp teeth.

Bright yellow eyes.

And an even sharper appetite.

When he smelled pigs nearby, he grinned.

"Dinner."

The wolf soon discovered the straw house.

He knocked loudly.

BOOM! BOOM! BOOM!

"Little pig, little pig, let me come in."

"No!"

replied the pig.

The wolf laughed.

"Then I'll huff and I'll puff and I'll blow your house down!"

The wolf inhaled deeply.

HUUUUUFF!

PUUUUUFF!

The straw house exploded into pieces.

The terrified pig ran away as fast as he could.

Fortunately, he escaped and hurried to his brother's wooden house.
