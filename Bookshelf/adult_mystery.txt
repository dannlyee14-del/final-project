| LOCKED ROOM|
|____________|

The Hawthorne Mansion stood alone on a hill overlooking the sea.

For more than a century, wealthy families, politicians, and celebrities had gathered there for exclusive events.

On a cold autumn evening, twelve guests attended a private dinner hosted by billionaire Arthur Hawthorne.

By midnight, one of them would be dead.

The evening began peacefully.

Guests enjoyed fine food and expensive wine while a storm raged outside.

Thunder shook the windows.

Rain battered the roof.

The old mansion seemed completely isolated from the world.

No one could leave.

No one could enter.

At 11:45 p.m., Arthur excused himself and entered his private library.

He told the guests he needed to review several business documents.

The room contained thousands of books, antique furniture, and a large stone fireplace.

Most importantly, it had only one entrance.

The guests remained in the dining hall.

Fifteen minutes later, a scream echoed through the mansion.

Everyone rushed toward the library.

The door was locked.

Arthur did not respond.

The butler used a spare key.

When the door finally opened, the guests froze.

Arthur Hawthorne was dead.

.fig
___________
| LIBRARY  |
|__________|
.figend

The room presented an impossible mystery.

All windows were locked from the inside.

The fireplace had not been used.

No secret passages were visible.

The victim sat motionless behind his desk.

There was no weapon.

No footprints.

No signs of struggle.

Only one clue remained.

Scratched into the wooden surface of the desk were three groups of symbols.

... --- ...

Several guests assumed they were random marks.

Detective Amelia Frost disagreed.

She had been invited to the dinner because of her reputation for solving difficult cases.

She immediately recognized the pattern.

SOS.

A distress signal.

Why would a dying man leave a distress signal moments before his death?

Amelia began interviewing the guests.

Each person claimed to have remained in the dining hall throughout the evening.

Their stories matched.

No one appeared suspicious.

Yet everyone possessed a possible motive.

Arthur Hawthorne had made many enemies.

His business rival hated him.

His nephew expected an inheritance.

A former employee blamed him for financial ruin.

Even some close friends held grudges.

The list of suspects continued growing.

.fig
_______________
| SUSPECT LIST |
|______________|
.figend

While examining the library, Amelia discovered another clue.

A sheet of paper hidden beneath a book.

Written on the page was a strange equation.

?50*2+100/5?

Most people would have ignored it.

Amelia did not.

Arthur loved puzzles.

He often hid information inside mathematical challenges.

She solved the equation.

The result pointed to a page number in a nearby journal.

Inside the journal she found a coded message.

Someone at the dinner had been blackmailing Arthur.

| JOURNAL   |

The investigation continued through the night.

Amelia carefully reconstructed every movement inside the mansion.

Hours later she noticed something unusual.

One guest repeatedly avoided discussing the mansion's renovation history.

That guest was Victor Lang.

An architect.

Several years earlier, Victor had supervised repairs throughout the property.

Including the library.

Amelia requested the renovation blueprints.

Victor hesitated.

That hesitation revealed everything.

| HIDDEN PLANS    |
|_________________|

The blueprints contained a secret.

A narrow maintenance shaft connected the library fireplace to an unused storage room below.

The opening was nearly invisible.

Most people would never notice it.

A skilled architect, however, knew exactly where it was located.

Victor finally confessed.

Years earlier Arthur had destroyed his career through fraud and deception.

Victor wanted revenge.

During the dinner, he entered the hidden passage before Arthur arrived in the library.

He confronted him.

An argument followed.

The confrontation became deadly.

Afterward Victor escaped through the same passage.

The locked room illusion remained intact.

The mystery seemed solved.

Then Amelia discovered one final surprise.

Arthur's SOS message had not referred to himself.

Before his death, Arthur had learned that another guest planned to commit a separate crime involving company funds.

The distress signal was a warning.

Not a plea for help.

Even while dying, Arthur attempted to expose the truth.

.fig
________________
| FINAL CLUE    |
|_______________|
.figend

By sunrise, police arrested Victor Lang.

Investigators later uncovered the financial crimes Arthur had attempted to reveal.

The Hawthorne Mansion returned to silence.

Guests departed.

The storm passed.

The locked room mystery became one of the most famous cases in the city's history.

Years later, visitors still discussed the impossible murder.

Many believed the case demonstrated the brilliance of Detective Amelia Frost.

Others believed it showed how dangerous secrets could become.

Amelia preferred a simpler explanation.

"No mystery is impossible," she often said.

"People only fail to notice the truth."
