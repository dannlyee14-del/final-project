Alice was beginning to get very tired of sitting by her sister on the bank, 
and of having nothing to do: once or twice she had peeped into the book 
her sister was reading, but it had no pictures or conversations in it, 
“and what is the use of a book,” thought Alice “without pictures or 
conversations?”

So she was considering in her own mind (as well as she could, for the hot 
day made her feel very sleepy and stupid), whether the pleasure of 
making a daisy-chain would be worth the trouble of getting up and 
picking the daisies, when suddenly a White Rabbit with pink eyes 
ran close by her.

There was nothing so VERY remarkable in that; nor did Alice think it so 
VERY much out of the way to hear the Rabbit say to itself, “Oh dear! 
Oh dear! I shall be late!” 

(When she thought it over afterwards, it occurred to her that she ought 
to have wondered at this, but at the time it all seemed quite natural); 
but when the Rabbit actually TOOK A WATCH OUT OF ITS WAISTCOAT-POCKET, 
and looked at it, and then hurried on, Alice started to her feet.

Alice followed the White Rabbit deeper into the tunnel.

The passage twisted and turned beneath the earth.

Soon she arrived at a strange hallway filled with tiny doors.

Each door had a number written above it.

Alice tried opening the first door.

Locked.

The second door.

Locked.

Then she noticed a golden plaque.

"A puzzle?" Alice wondered.

The numbers shimmered.

Moments later, the answer appeared.

The smallest door slowly opened.

Beyond it lay a garden unlike anything she had ever seen.

Flowers sang songs.

Butterflies glowed in rainbow colors.

Even the clouds seemed alive.

Alice looked around.

The path eventually led her to a forest.

The trees whispered to one another.

Suddenly she heard a familiar sound.

... --- ...

Alice stopped walking.

The sound repeated again.

... --- ...

The Cheshire Cat appeared on a nearby branch.

"Do you know what that means?" asked the cat.

Alice thought carefully.

"It sounds like a signal."

The cat smiled.

"Very good."

"It is a message from someone trapped inside Wonderland."

The sound echoed again.

... --- ...

Alice followed it through the forest.

Eventually she reached an ancient tower.

At the top of the tower sat a mechanical rabbit.

Unlike the White Rabbit, this one was made entirely of brass and gears.

Its eyes glowed blue.

A screen on its chest displayed another puzzle.

?100/4?

The machine remained motionless.

Alice solved it immediately.

The gears began turning.

CLICK.

CLACK.

WHIRRR.

The rabbit sprang to life.

"Thank you," said the machine.

"I have been asleep for many years."

The rabbit handed Alice a crystal compass.

"This will guide you to the Queen's maze."

Alice accepted the gift.

Soon she arrived at a giant hedge maze.

The entrance contained one final challenge.

Alice solved the equation.

The maze opened.

Inside she discovered a hidden library containing every story ever written in Wonderland.

Books stretched endlessly in every direction.

Some told stories of the past.

Others described the future.

And one book contained her own adventure.

Alice carefully opened it.

The final page was blank.

As she watched, words slowly appeared.

"The adventure is only beginning."
